History
Guglielmo Marconi, 1st Marquis of Marconi was an Italian inventor and electrical engineer, known for his pioneering work on long-distance radio transmission and for his development of Marconi's law and a radio telegraph system. Marconi succeeded in making a commercial success of radio by innovating and building on the work of previous experimenters and physicists. In 1929 the King of Italy ennobled Marconi as a Marchese (marquis).

    Important Dates
  • 1897 - Founds The Wireless Telegraph & Signal Company in Britain
  • 17 December 1902 - First trans-Atlantic wireless transmission
  • 1909 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Karl Braun)
  • 15 April 1912 - RMS Titanic uses Marconi wireless to request rescue assistance
  • 7 May 1915 - RMS Lusitania uses Marconi wireless to request rescue assistance
